# Nexus Blackout Narrative Reboot

## Source Direction

Reference: GameSpot's roundup of story-driven games emphasizes clear stakes, memorable places, character pressure, and player agency. The takeaway for Nexus is not to copy any plot, but to stop using abstract verbs. Players need to understand who is in danger, why the clock matters, and what each objective physically does.

## Working Premise

Nexus Blackout is a short arcade rescue mission on Deck 7 of a locked-down station. Survivors are trapped behind a blast door. Security drones are treating every moving target as hostile. The operators have one run to restore power, open the door, and call the evacuation lift.

## Narrative Rules

- Use concrete nouns: power cells, blast doors, override terminals, rescue lift, drones.
- Every objective must answer: what changed in the world when this is completed?
- Co-op copy should explain role split, not just say "collaborate."
- Avoid abstract product language such as route, prototype, idea, frame, share, or spark.
- Cinematics should preview playable space and mission risk, then hand control back quickly.

## Playfield And Cinematic Standard

- The arena should feel like a station deck, not a small test grid. Missions use a larger floor plan with service lanes, bulkheads, cover, terminals, and extraction space.
- Each stage must reconfigure the level so the player understands a new physical problem: follow the ordered power-cell route, cross the maze to override terminals, then survive at the lift beacon.
- Stage transitions should briefly take control of the camera, show the next deck layout, name the next objective, then return control.
- The ending should resolve the mission with extraction confirmation instead of dropping straight into a score state.
- Avoid decorative props that block the camera, hide the player, or read as unusable fake models.

## Mission Arc

1. Power Cell Route
   Follow the bright amber cell route in order so evacuation systems can restart.

2. Override Chain
   Activate Alpha, then Omega, using maze walls and cover to break seeker line of sight.

3. Extraction Hold
   Hold the rescue beacon while drones converge until extraction locks.
